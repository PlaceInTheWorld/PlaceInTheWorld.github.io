Am I lazy for not wanting to find a "real" job?

My time in El Nido didn’t start off great — I got food poisoning and spent days recovering. But that downtime gave me space to think about work, gap years, and what I actually want from the next phase of life. This post less about travel and more about trying to make sense of what comes next.

Our visit to El Nido has been transformative for me.

I got a really bad case of food poisoning, and it took me over a week to recover. The first few days, I had no energy at all, and I barely remember how those days even passed. But gradually, I started feeling better, and eventually, I even found a new sense of motivation to be productive.

In El Nido, something has shifted. This place — part of the backpacking route and full of people exploring — made me reflect on the idea of a "gap year." Not every year can be a gap year, after all. If you have too many gap years, they stop being a “gap” and just become your life.

I started looking for a job online—something I could do from home or maybe once I arrived on one of the nearby islands. I found a few listings that seemed interesting and saved them to my “liked options.” But I just couldn’t imagine going into one of those jobs and loving it.

The jobs that sounded even somewhat interesting were out of my reach because I didn’t have the qualifications or experience. And the ones I could potentially do seemed really boring to me. The thought of coming back from the Philippines and jumping straight into the most basic, uninteresting job felt so heavy. After just a few days of searching online, I realized I don’t want that kind of life.

My first reaction to this realization was to assume I’m just lazy. If I don’t want to go to work, maybe it means I don’t want to do anything — just lie around and let time slip through my fingers. But that’s not at all true. I have so many things I want to do; they just don’t involve being told when to wake up, how to spend my day, or why I can’t earn more because someone else might do the same job for less.

It felt like nothing would have changed. I’ve been on this once-in-a-lifetime trip, enjoying every second, having these big realizations, feeling inspired and creative. I feel like I’ve changed so much—so why should I go back to the same life I had before? Back to university, to a random job that brings no real joy?

We went for a 1 hour walk from our hotel to a nearby beach, Vanilla beach. 

So, I decided to give another idea a chance. I understand that sometimes we need to do things we don’t like (jobs that pay the bills) in order to do the things we love (travel, read, create). But I don’t feel like this next year is meant to be about survival. I want to try working on a few ideas I’ve had, to see if I can start making money doing something I actually care about.

I have about four more months left in this gap year. If it all goes badly, I can just go back to a regular job in September like nothing happened. But I’d regret not trying.

So that’s what El Nido was for me. Mostly, it was about recovering from food poisoning — but also visiting the beach, exploring the town, hiking to other beaches, and having a lot of fun adventures. At the same time, I spent hours reflecting on my projects, trying to put into words and plans the things that I enjoy and that bring me happiness — and maybe could bring in some income too.

I'm going to try and document this journey and hopefully by the time University starts, I can look back and see that I have come a long way.
